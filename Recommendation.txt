Underweight
. Add healthy fats to meals and snacks, such as avocado, nuts, nut butter, and olive oil.
. Include energy-dense foods like whole grains (oats, quinoa, brown rice), potatoes, and legumes.
. Incorporate lean sources of protein like chicken breast, turkey, fish, eggs, tofu, and legumes.
. Include dairy products like milk, yogurt, and cheese (if not lactose intolerant).
. Opt for complex carbohydrates like whole wheat bread, whole grain pasta, sweet potatoes, and brown rice.
. Include fruits and vegetables for added vitamins, minerals, and fiber.
. Make smoothies with milk or yogurt, fruits, and nut butter.
. Snack on nuts, seeds, and dried fruits and other high-calorie snacks like granola bars, energy bars, or trail mix.
. Ensure regular meals and snacks throughout the day to provide a steady intake of calories.
. Consider having smaller, frequent meals instead of large meals.
. Drink adequate water throughout the day to stay hydrated.

Normal weight
. Include a variety of fruits and vegetables in your meals for essential vitamins, minerals, and fiber.
. Opt for whole fruits instead of fruit juices to get the benefits of fiber.
. Choose whole grains like brown rice, quinoa, whole wheat bread, and whole grain pasta for added fiber and nutrients.
. Include lean sources of protein such as chicken breast, turkey, fish, tofu, legumes, and low-fat dairy products.
. Incorporate sources of healthy fats like avocados, nuts, seeds, and olive oil in moderation.
. Ensure your meals contain a combination of protein, carbohydrates, and fats.
. Include a variety of food groups to meet your nutritional needs.
. Be mindful of portion sizes to maintain a balanced intake of calories.
. Drink plenty of water throughout the day to stay hydrated.
. Pay attention to your hunger and fullness cues, and eat when you're hungry and stop when you're satisfied.
. Engage in regular exercise or physical activity to maintain overall health and well-being.

Overweight
. Include a variety of fruits and vegetables in your meals and snacks, they are low in calories and high in fiber, vitamins, and minerals.
. Opt for whole grain options like brown rice, whole wheat bread, and whole grain pasta.
. Include lean sources of protein such as skinless poultry, fish, tofu, legumes, and low-fat dairy products.
. Reduce consumption of processed foods high in added sugars, unhealthy fats, and empty calories.
. Limit sugary snacks, soda, candy, sugary cereals, and processed snacks.
. Pay attention to portion sizes and avoid oversized portions.
. Use smaller plates, bowls, and cups to help control the amount of food you eat.
. Include healthy fats in your diet such as avocados, nuts, seeds, and olive oil, however, be mindful of portion sizes as fats are calorie-dense.
. Increase intake of fiber-rich foods like fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes can help you feel fuller for longer.
. Drink plenty of water throughout the day to stay hydrated and help manage cravings.
. Combine a healthy diet with regular exercise or physical activity to support weight management.
